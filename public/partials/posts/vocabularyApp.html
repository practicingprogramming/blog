Object-oriented design is something every professional software developer should be good at, but I find this skill difficult to improve. Despite writing lots of code at work, I rarely have an opportunity to practice my OOD. I blame the frameworks we use: they are too good and do all the object-oriented design for me. I could not find a lot of OOD exercises on the Internet either: they usually are either too simple, or too open ended, or too unrealistic.

Because of this, in order to improve my OOD skills, I decided to design my own exercises. They should:

  • Be realistic, describing a real world issue.
  • Simple enough so I can implement them within a few hours.
  • Have few or better no external dependencies.
  • Ideally, but not necessarily, allow a good usage of a common design pattern.

Vocabulary App

The first exercise I designed is a simple command-line application for memorizing new words called "Vocabulary".

The app should:

  • Read words and their definitions from a file.
  • Choose what word to ask based on a strategy passed as an argument.
  • Ask the user to guess the word by its definition.
  • Tell if the answer is correct.
  • Record the question and the answer to a log.

Example:
Question: Woman
Hombre
Incorrect! Correct answer: Mujer.

Assume that each question has exactly one correct answer.

The app should support different modes/strategies for selecting which questions to ask. Possible strategies include:

  • Random - ask a random word from the dictionary.
  • Ask the least frequently asked word.
  • Ask the word with the worst correct answer rate.
However, we want the app to be extensible and adding new strategies simple. Current strategy is passed as a command line argument.

My Design

  • I tried to isolate user interactions (QuizRunner) from persistence (Dictionary and AnswerLog) and from everything else.
  • Strategies are implemented using, unsurprisingly, Strategy design pattern.
  • I don't know what sort of strategies I may need in the future, but it's unlikely that they will depend on anything other than Dictionary and AnswerLog.

Implementation

I chose to write this in Ruby. The source code of my implementation is on Github. I'm still working on it at the moment. In the future, I'm going to use it for practicing testing applications with Cucumber.

Final Notes
